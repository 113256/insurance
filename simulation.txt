You: "Can you please explain the difference between term life insurance and whole life insurance, and which one would be more beneficial for a student like me?"
Officer: Absolutely! Term life insurance and whole life insurance serve different purposes, and the choice between them often depends on your specific circumstances and goals.  Term life insurance provides coverage for a specific period, typically ranging from 10 to 30 years. It offers a death benefit to your beneficiaries if you pass away during the term of the policy. It's usually more affordable compared to whole life insurance because it doesn't have a cash value component or investment features. Term life insurance is often chosen to cover temporary needs, such as paying off a mortgage, providing for dependents, or covering educational expenses.  Whole life insurance, on the other hand, provides coverage for your entire life. It accumulates cash value over time, which you can access through loans or withdrawals while you're alive. This type of insurance typically has higher premiums but offers lifelong protection and a savings component.
